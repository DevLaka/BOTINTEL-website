Bot Intel for, Intelligent Service Robots

From UNKNOWN to KNOWN.

Abstract

Intelligent service robots are currently being developed to provide services and assistance in different sectors including domestic and household context. Typically, the service task of a domestic service robot involves direct interaction with humans. Humans typically express their ideas through voice communication. However, communication through natural language is imprecise because it tends to contain uncertain and unknown information. Therefore, understanding uncertain terms contained in natural language is a crucial capability that an intelligent service robot should possess. So that it would ultimately increase user satisfaction while reducing the communication barriers between human users and their domestic robotic assistants. Hence, this project which is named as IntelBot is aimed at developing a methodology to cope with uncertain and unknown words contained in a natural language command given to a domestic service robot. This project consists of four components. They are interpretation of uncertain terms related to the speed of movements and uncertain terms related to time, development of ontology-based robotic knowledge for unknown words/objects interpretation, implementation of image processing technique for precise object detection and implementing a user-friendly interface for ROS(Robot Operating System). In brief, the proposed system has the ability to interpret uncertain commands related to speed such as “go very fast” and the uncertain commands related to time such as “go later”. Additionally, if the robot is instructed to identify an object which is regarded to be unknown, as an example “cup" it has the ability to interpret and identify that particular object. And for the entire system, a user-friendly interface is developed for the easy control of the robot and for the demonstration of the functionalities.
